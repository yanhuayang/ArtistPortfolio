Resilient Resistance

cast iron, latex, resin bonded sand, steel, 2019

The goal of this work was to capture the visible stretch seen in surgical tubing and athletic resistance bands in iron. The original part was made of surgical tubing and for a while now this rubber tubing has represented the ligaments and tendons in my body. By casting them in iron, it feels as though I am hardening them as a strange attempt to protect my own tendons, specifically the ones in my right ankle. Eventually, the water based latex will begin to rust the iron, allowing a new color and texture to creep up, alluding to time and aging. This work references my time in physical therapy for an injury in which reoccurrence feels inevitable.
